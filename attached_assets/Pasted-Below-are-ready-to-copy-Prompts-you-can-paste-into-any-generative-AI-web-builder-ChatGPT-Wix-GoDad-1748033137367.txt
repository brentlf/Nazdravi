1 · Global footer / mini-imprint
Prompt:
“Draft a concise website footer (max 150 words) for an online dietitian practice.
Context: The business is physically established in the Netherlands, notified under the Dutch Wtza, but NOT in the BIG register; the dietitian is HCPC-registered (UK) and HPCSA-registered (South Africa) and sometimes provides Czech-language care via the EU temporary-services scheme.
Must contain, in plain English:

Full practice name and address in the Netherlands.

KvK number [KVK_NUMBER].

VAT status (‘health-care services VAT-exempt in NL; VAT may apply to non-care products’).

Professional titles with registers and numbers (HCPC, HPCSA).

One-line notice of Wtza notification.

English-only service statement + link to Czech summary PDF.

Complaints-officer e-mail and disputes-committee link.

Emergency disclaimer (“No emergency services; call local emergency number”).

Copyright year.
Keep it legally precise but readable.”

2 · Czech-language micro-summary page
Prompt (Czech):
„Napiš jednostránkový český souhrn právních informací pro online poradnu klinické výživy se sídlem v Nizozemsku. Stručně vysvětli:
• že služby jsou poskytovány ‘na dočasném a příležitostném základě’ dle § 36a zákona 18/2004 Sb.
• že dietolog má uznanou odbornou kvalifikaci (HCPC UK, HPCSA SA).
• že jde o soukromou službu, úhrada z veřejného pojištění není možná.

• odkaz na plné anglické obchodní podmínky a zásady ochrany osobních údajů.
Rozsah max 250 slov.“

3 · “About / Professional Credentials” section
Prompt:
“Write an “About the Dietitian” section (~300 words).
Must include:
• Overview of qualifications: BSc in Dietetics (give UK university as [UNI]), MSc [if any], CPD.
• Registration numbers: HCPC [HCPC_NO], HPCSA [HPCSA_NO].
• Statement that the protected Dutch title ‘diëtist’ falls under Article 34 Wet BIG and therefore no BIG-register entry is required.
• Practice languages: English (primary), Czech (on request).
• Mission statement focused on evidence-based nutrition, patient autonomy, cross-cultural sensitivity.
Tone: warm and jargon-free.”

4 · Informed-consent & intake form copy
Prompt:
“Generate the text for a two-step online intake form for a tele-dietetics consultation.
Step 1 – key consents (checkboxes, all required):

I confirm I can read, speak and understand English OR Czech.

I understand services are private, not emergency care.

I consent to video consultation and electronic record-keeping under GDPR.

I acknowledge results vary and no specific weight-loss amount is guaranteed.

SA clients only: I understand local follow-up with a physician may be required (HPCSA Booklet 10).
Step 2 – health screening (mandatory inputs): age, height, weight, chronic conditions, current medication, recent blood-tests (Y/N), and GP contact.
End with a ‘Proceed to Booking’ button.
Make wording patient-friendly; limit to 400 words total.”

5 · Terms & Conditions (T&C) prompt
Prompt (long-form):
“Draft full Terms & Conditions for an online dietitian service (approx. 2 000 words; numbered sections; simple language).
Include these headings and content cues:

Definitions (Provider, Client, Services).

Scope of Services – lifestyle & medical nutrition; no invasive procedures.

Geographic Eligibility – residents of UK, South Africa, Czech Republic, Netherlands expats; English or Czech speakers only.

Professional Status & Governing Law – Netherlands law applies; cross-border recognition paragraphs for UK (HCPC), SA (HPCSA), CZ (§ 36a).

Booking, Fees, VAT (mention health-care VAT exemption & 21 % VAT for non-care products).

14-Day EU right of withdrawal & digital-content waiver tick-box.

Privacy & Data Protection – GDPR reference; link to full privacy notice.

Medical Disclaimer – general advice, no guaranteed outcomes.

Client Obligations – accurate health info; physician coordination for high-risk cases.

Complaints & Disputes – contact [EMAIL]; external committee [NAME], then Dutch courts / ČOI for Czech consumers.

Liability Limit – within statutory limits; no liability for indirect loss.

Changes to T&C & notice method.
Place-holders: [PRACTICE_NAME], [ADDRESS], [KVK], [VAT_NO], [COMPLAINTS_OFFICER_EMAIL], [DISPUTES_COMMITTEE_URL].
Ensure plain-English drafting, no “whereas”, aim for Flesch-Kincaid grade 8.”

6 · Privacy & Cookies notice prompt
Prompt:
“Create an EU-GDPR-compliant Privacy & Cookies Notice (≈1 500 words) for a tele-health website storing special-category data.
Must cover:
• Data controller details ([PRACTICE_NAME], Netherlands address).
• Purposes & legal bases: medical diagnosis/treatment (Art 9 2(h)), legitimate interests (quality monitoring), consent (marketing).
• Data processors: video-platform [NAME], scheduling app [NAME]; each EU-based.
• Retention: 10 years for medical records (WGBO).
• International transfers: none outside EEA unless SCCs in place.
• Patients’ rights: access, rectification, deletion (unless health-record retention overrides), complaint to Dutch AP or local DPA.
• Cookies: strictly necessary vs analytics (opt-in banner with granular controls).
Tone: clear, headings & bullet lists.”
